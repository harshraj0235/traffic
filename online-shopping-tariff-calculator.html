How Tariffs Affect Online Shopping in

Online shopping has been fundamentally impacted by the 2025-2026 tariff increases. Whether you shop on Amazon, eBay, Walmart.com, or international platforms, tariffs are built into the prices of most products you buy.

Do I Pay Tariffs on Amazon?

Yes — indirectly. Products sold on Amazon that are imported (which includes most electronics, clothing, toys, and home goods) have tariff costs built into their retail price. You don't see a separate "tariff" line item at checkout, but the price you pay includes the tariff cost that the importer/seller has absorbed and passed on.

Platform-by-Platform Guide

  • Amazon: Most products already have tariffs priced in. Look for "Made in USA" labels to find tariff-free options.
  • eBay: Domestic sellers have tariffs already built in. International sellers may ship directly, triggering customs duties.
  • Walmart: Similar to Amazon — tariffs are built into retail prices on imported goods.
  • Shein/Temu: Direct-from-China shipping faces full tariffs and postal fees. See our dedicated Shein/Temu calculator.
  • AliExpress: Chinese sellers ship direct — expect tariffs/postal fees on every order.

Online Shopping Tariff FAQ

Common questions about tariffs on online purchases.

Do I pay tariffs on Amazon purchases?+

Yes, indirectly. Tariffs are paid by importers and built into the retail price. You won't see a separate tariff charge, but products with imported components cost more than they would without tariffs.

Why don't I see tariffs at checkout?+

For domestic purchases, tariffs are already factored into the retail price. You only see explicit customs/duty charges when ordering directly from international sellers who ship across borders.

Is it cheaper to buy from domestic sellers?+

Generally yes, because domestic sellers have already imported goods in bulk (paying tariffs at scale) and can often negotiate better rates. Direct-from-China purchases face full tariffs plus postal fees on each package.

How do I find tariff-free products online?+

Products manufactured in the USA don't carry import tariffs. Look for "Made in USA" labels. On Amazon, you can filter by "Domestic" sellers. Some product categories also have temporary tariff exemptions.

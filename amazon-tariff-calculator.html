Does Amazon Include Tariffs in Its Prices?

Yes. If you are buying a product on Amazon.com that is shipped from within the US (like Amazon Prime items), the tariff cost is already embedded into the listed price.

Amazon sellers who import goods from China, Vietnam, or elsewhere must pay the US import tariff when the inventory arrives at the US border, long before you click "Add to Cart." To maintain their profit margins, sellers raise their retail prices on Amazon.

📌 How Amazon Tariffs Work

  • FBA Sellers: Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA) sellers pay tariffs upfront when importing bulk inventory. They pass this cost to you in the list price.
  • Third-Party Direct Shippers: Sellers who ship directly from overseas to your door are now subject to tariffs because the $800 de minimis loophole was suspended in 2025.
  • Amazon Basics: Amazon's own private label goods are predominantly manufactured in Asia and are subject to the same high tariff rates.

⚠️ The End of De Minimis on Amazon

Before 2025, third-party Amazon sellers could ship items directly from China to your door duty-free if the value was under $800. With this loophole suspended for Chinese shipments, these direct-ship items are now hit with the full 54%+ combined tariff rate upon entering the US, resulting in massive price hikes for cheap electronics, clothing, and home goods on Amazon.

How to Save Money on Amazon

  • Check "Country of Origin": Scroll down to the "Product Details" section. An item made in Mexico (25% tariff) might be cheaper than the exact same item made in China (54% tariff).
  • Filter for "Made in USA": Products manufactured domestically face no import tariffs.
  • Look for Refurbished: Amazon Renewed products are already in the country and avoid new import tariffs.

Amazon Tariff FAQ

Do I have to pay customs fees when my Amazon package arrives?+

Generally, no. If you are buying on Amazon.com and the item is shipped domestically (like Prime items), the seller has already paid the tariff, and it is included in the price you see. If you are buying an item that ships directly from a foreign country, Amazon may collect an "Import Fees Deposit" at checkout, so you still won't pay at the door.

Why did the price of cheap electronics on Amazon go up so much?+

Two reasons: The suspension of the $800 de minimis exemption, and the combined 54%+ tariff rate on Chinese goods. Previously, a $20 charging cable shipped from Shenzhen avoided tariffs. Now, it faces the full tariff rate, forcing the seller to raise the price significantly.

Are Amazon Basics products affected by tariffs?+

Yes. Amazon Basics products are largely manufactured in China, Vietnam, and other Asian countries. Amazon, as the importer of record, pays the tariffs and adjusts the retail prices accordingly.

How can I tell where an Amazon product is made before buying?+

Scroll down to the "Product Details" or "Product Information" section on the listing page. Look for the "Country of Origin" field. Note that some sellers obscure this information, but US law requires country of origin marking on physical products.

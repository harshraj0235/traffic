How to Save Money Despite Tariffs

The average household pays $1,500-$3,000/year more due to tariffs. But there are smart strategies to minimize your tariff burden. Here are 15+ actionable tips to keep more money in your pocket.

Your Complete Tariff Savings Playbook

Strategy 1: Buy American-Made

Products manufactured in the USA don't carry import tariffs. While not everything is available domestically, many categories have strong US-made options: furniture, some clothing brands (American Giant, Todd Shelton), certain tools (some DeWalt, Snap-on), and many food products.

Strategy 2: Shop Smart on Electronics

Electronics face some of the highest tariffs (54%+ from China). To save: buy refurbished from Apple or Best Buy (tariff already paid), wait for sales events when retailers absorb costs, or consider products assembled outside China (Samsung from South Korea, some Apple products from India).

Strategy 3: Check Country of Origin

The same product may be manufactured in different countries with vastly different tariff rates. A shirt from the UK (10% tariff) costs less in tariffs than the same shirt from China (45%+). Look at product labels before buying.

Strategy 4: Time Your Purchases

Retailers holding pre-tariff inventory may offer better prices. Major sales events (Prime Day, Black Friday) often see retailers absorbing tariff costs to maintain volume. Trade deal announcements can also temporarily reduce rates.

Strategy 5: Go Secondhand

Secondhand goods purchased domestically (ThredUp, Poshmark, Facebook Marketplace, thrift stores) don't incur additional tariffs. The original tariff was already paid when the item was first imported. This is especially effective for clothing, electronics, and furniture.

US Import Duty Guide for Personal Imports

Returning Traveler Exemptions

US residents returning from abroad can bring back up to $800 worth of goods duty-free (as long as you haven't used this exemption in the past 30 days). The next $1,000 above that is taxed at a flat 3% rate. Values above $1,800 face the standard tariff rates for each product category.

Ordering From Abroad

If you order products from international sellers that ship directly to the US, customs duties apply based on the product type and country of origin. For Chinese shipments, the de minimis exemption ($800 duty-free) has been suspended — see our de minimis calculator for details.

📋 Key Import Duty Facts

  • $800 duty-free exemption for returning travelers (every 30 days)
  • De minimis ($800 duty-free for mailed goods) suspended for China
  • Duty rates vary by product and country of origin
  • CBP Form 6059B required for declaring items at the border
  • Undeclared items risk seizure and penalties
  • Some items (certain foods, plants, animals) are restricted

Import Duty FAQ

How much can I bring back duty-free?+

US residents can bring back up to $800 worth of goods duty-free every 30 days. Family members can combine their exemptions. The next $1,000 is taxed at a flat 3%.

Do I have to declare everything at customs?+

Yes, you must declare all items acquired abroad, even if they're under the $800 exemption. Failure to declare can result in seizure and penalties.

What is an HTS code?+

HTS (Harmonized Tariff Schedule) codes classify products for customs purposes. Each product type has a specific code that determines the exact duty rate. Our calculator uses category-level estimates; for precise rates, look up your specific HTS code.

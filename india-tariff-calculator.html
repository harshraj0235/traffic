US-India Tariffs Explained (2026)

India is a major supplier of pharmaceuticals, textiles, and increasingly, electronics (as companies diversify supply chains away from China). The US has imposed a 26% reciprocal tariff on goods imported from India.

📌 India Tariff Key Facts

  • Reciprocal rate: 26% on most Indian goods
  • Pharmaceuticals: India supplies ~40% of generic drugs in the US
  • Textiles: Significant impact on clothing and home textiles
  • Tech manufacturing: Affects iPhones and other electronics recently moved to Indian assembly
  • Diamonds: India is a major hub for diamond cutting and polishing

Most Affected Indian Imports

The categories most impacted by the US-India tariff include:

  • Pharmaceuticals & Medicine: As a leading global provider of generic drugs, the 26% tariff significantly affects healthcare costs and out-of-pocket expenses for US consumers.
  • Textiles & Apparel: Clothing, bed sheets, and towels manufactured in India face higher retail prices.
  • Electronics (e.g., iPhones): Apple has shifted significant iPhone production to India. These devices are subject to the 26% reciprocal tariff when imported into the US.
  • Spices & Agriculture: Indian exports like rice, spices (pepper, turmeric), and tea face increased costs.

🔄 Supply Chain Shifts

Many US companies moved manufacturing from China to India to avoid Section 301 tariffs. While India's 26% tariff is lower than the 54%+ combined rate on Chinese goods, it still represents a significant cost increase compared to pre-2018 levels.

India Tariff FAQ

Why is there a 26% tariff on goods from India?+

The 26% tariff is a "reciprocal" tariff, calculated based on the average tariff rate that India applies to American goods imported into India.

Will this affect the price of my prescription drugs?+

Yes, likely. India supplies about 40% of the generic drugs sold in the US. A 26% import tax increases the cost for distributors, which is often passed down to pharmacies and consumers.

Are iPhones made in India subject to this tariff?+

Yes. iPhones and other electronics assembled in India and imported to the US face the 26% reciprocal tariff. Apple and retailers may absorb some costs, but retail prices generally reflect the increase.

Is the US negotiating a trade deal with India?+

Trade negotiations are ongoing, but historically, India has maintained high domestic tariffs to protect its own industries, making a comprehensive free trade agreement challenging to reach.

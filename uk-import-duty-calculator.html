US-UK Tariffs Explained

The United Kingdom currently enjoys the most favorable tariff status among major US trading partners. Unlike the EU (20%), China (54%+), or Canada/Mexico (25%), the UK only faces the 10% global baseline tariff implemented under Section 122.

This makes importing British goods significantly cheaper than importing the same goods from the EU or Asia. A potential US-UK free trade agreement could eliminate this 10% tariff entirely.

📋 UK Import Facts

  • 10% tariff rate applies to almost all UK imports
  • No additional reciprocal tariffs are currently applied to the UK
  • Cheaper to source goods from the UK than the EU (20%)
  • Scotch whisky and British fashion remain popular, lower-tariff imports

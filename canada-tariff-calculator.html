The 25% Canada Tariff Explained

In early 2025, the US invoked the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA) to impose a 25% across-the-board tariff on all imports from Canada and Mexico, citing border security concerns. This supersedes the duty-free provisions of the USMCA (formerly NAFTA) trade agreement.

Most Affected Consumer Goods

  • Lumber & Furniture: Canada is the #1 supplier of softwood lumber to the US. The 25% tariff has drastically increased home building costs and furniture prices.
  • Vehicles: Many US brands assemble vehicles in Canada (e.g., Chrysler Pacifica, Ford Edge, some Honda Civics). These face the 25% import tariff.
  • Food & Grocery: Canadian beef, pork, and seafood are significantly more expensive.

📋 Canada Tariff Facts

  • Flat 25% tariff on ALL Canadian imports
  • Overrides USMCA duty-free trade provisions
  • Same tariff applies equally to Mexico
  • Significant impact on US housing market (lumber)

Electronics from China

54% – 145% Tariff Rate

Smartphones, laptops, tablets, gaming consoles, and accessories. China tariffs include the 20% baseline + 34% reciprocal tariff + additional Section 301 duties up to 100% on specific tech categories.

Automobiles & Parts

25% – 50% Tariff Rate

All imported vehicles face a 25% tariff effective April 2025, with auto parts at 25% from May 2025. This applies regardless of country of origin, including Canada, Mexico, Japan, and the EU.

New Car: +$4,000-12,000 Parts: +25%

2025-2026 US Tariff Timeline

A chronological breakdown of major tariff changes affecting consumers.

Feb 4, 2025

China Tariff Increase Begins

10% additional tariff imposed on all Chinese imports under IEEPA authority, on top of existing Section 301 tariffs.

Mar 4, 2025

China Tariffs Doubled to 20%

Additional IEEPA tariff on China raised from 10% to 20%, citing fentanyl-related emergency.

Apr 2, 2025

Section 122 Global Tariff

10% baseline tariff on ALL imports from ALL countries. Higher "reciprocal" tariffs announced for specific nations.

Apr 9, 2025

China Tariffs Escalate to 145%

Combined tariff rate on Chinese goods reaches 145% after multiple escalations. 90-day pause on other reciprocal tariffs.

May 2, 2025

De Minimis Exemption Suspended

$800 duty-free threshold for small packages eliminated for China. All Shein, Temu, and AliExpress orders now subject to full tariffs or flat fees.

May 14, 2025

US-China 90-Day Deal

Tariffs temporarily reduced: US lowers China tariffs to 30% (from 145%), China lowers US tariffs to 10% (from 125%). 90-day negotiation window.

June 2026

Current Status

10% global baseline remains. China effective rates vary 30-54%+ depending on product category. Auto tariffs at 25%. De minimis suspension continues.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about how the 2025-2026 tariffs affect you.

What are tariffs and why do they affect my prices? +

Tariffs are taxes imposed on imported goods by the US government. When tariffs increase, importers pay more to bring goods into the country, and these costs are almost always passed on to consumers through higher retail prices. Studies show that 90-100% of tariff costs are ultimately borne by American consumers and businesses, not by foreign countries.

How accurate is this calculator? +

Our calculator provides estimates based on current published tariff schedules and category-level rates. Actual tariffs can vary based on the specific HTS (Harmonized Tariff Schedule) classification of each product, which can include thousands of sub-categories. Our estimates are designed to give consumers a realistic approximation of their tariff burden. For precise duty calculations on specific products, consult a customs broker.

What is the de minimis rule change and how does it affect me? +

Previously, packages worth under $800 could enter the US duty-free under the "de minimis" exemption. Starting May 2, 2025, this exemption was suspended for goods from China (and later expanded). This means small online orders from sites like Shein, Temu, AliExpress, and other Chinese retailers now face either full tariff rates or flat postal fees of $75-150 per package. This is a massive change for budget shoppers.

Are there any products exempt from the new tariffs? +

Some product categories have temporary exemptions or reduced rates. Pharmaceuticals and certain critical minerals have partial exemptions. Some electronics had temporary relief that has since expired. The tariff landscape is constantly evolving, with exemptions being granted and revoked. Our calculator uses the most current effective rates as of June 2026.

How much is the average American family paying in extra tariffs? +

According to multiple economic analyses, the average American household is paying approximately $1,500 to $3,000 more per year due to the 2025-2026 tariff increases. Lower-income households are disproportionately affected because they spend a higher percentage of their income on consumer goods. The impact varies significantly based on spending habits, with heavy online shoppers and those buying electronics or vehicles seeing the largest increases.

Will these tariffs be permanent? +

The tariff landscape is fluid and subject to ongoing trade negotiations. The 10% global baseline tariff is currently in effect with no announced expiration. China-specific tariffs have already been adjusted multiple times (from 145% to 30% after the May 2025 deal). Trade deals with individual countries could reduce or eliminate tariffs. We recommend checking back regularly as our calculator is updated when rates change.

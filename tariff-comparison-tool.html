Why Sourcing Matters

The US tariff system is highly variable based on country of origin. A product's tariff rate is not just based on what it is, but where it was made. This creates massive price disparities for identical products.

The "China Plus One" Strategy

Because China faces the highest US tariff rates (30-54%+), many companies have adopted a "China Plus One" sourcing strategy — moving production to countries like Vietnam, India, or Mexico. However, as our comparison tool shows, these countries also face significant tariffs (e.g., Vietnam at 46% for clothing).

Finding Tariff-Free Options

The only way to completely avoid US import tariffs is to buy domestically-produced goods. Products marked "Made in USA" or "Product of USA" are entirely exempt from import tariffs. However, products "Assembled in USA" from imported parts may still carry some tariff costs built into the component prices.

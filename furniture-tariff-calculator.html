Furniture Tariffs Explained

The furniture industry is deeply affected by two main tariff actions:

  • China Tariffs (45%+): China is the world's largest furniture exporter. Wayfair, Ashley Furniture, and Amazon Basics source heavily from China. These products face massive tariff markups.
  • Canada Tariffs (25%): The US relies on Canada for softwood lumber. The 25% IEEPA tariff on Canada directly increases the cost of raw materials for US domestic furniture manufacturers, raising prices across the board.
  • Vietnam (46%): Many companies shifted production from China to Vietnam to avoid tariffs, only to be hit by the 46% reciprocal tariff on Vietnamese goods.

To save money: Look for domestically manufactured furniture (though lumber costs are up), buy second-hand (tariff-free), or seek out brands sourcing from lower-tariff regions.

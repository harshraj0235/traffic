How Tariffs Affect Clothing Prices in

Clothing is one of the most tariff-impacted consumer categories. The US imports 97% of its clothing, and apparel already had some of the highest pre-existing tariff rates (12-32%) before the 2025 increases.

Brand-by-Brand Impact

  • Shein / Temu (China): 45%+ effective rate. The de minimis suspension means no more duty-free small packages.
  • Nike (Vietnam/Indonesia): 46-32% tariff rate depending on factory location. Athletic shoes face additional existing duties.
  • H&M (Bangladesh/China): 37-45% rate. Budget basics hit hardest.
  • Zara (China/India/Bangladesh): 26-45% depending on where each item is manufactured.
  • Lululemon (Vietnam): 46% reciprocal tariff on Vietnamese goods.
  • Gap / Old Navy (Vietnam/Bangladesh): 37-46% rate. Budget family clothing significantly affected.

📋 Key Clothing Tariff Facts

  • The US imports 97% of clothing sold domestically
  • Clothing already had 12-32% pre-existing tariffs before 2025
  • New tariffs compound on top of existing rates
  • China-made clothing: 45%+ effective rate
  • Vietnam-made clothing: up to 46% reciprocal tariff
  • Bangladesh-made clothing: 37% reciprocal tariff
  • Thrift stores and secondhand shops offer tariff-free alternatives

Clothing Tariff FAQ

Questions about tariffs on clothing and fashion.

Why are clothing tariffs so high?+

Clothing has historically had some of the highest US tariff rates (12-32% even before 2025). The new tariffs compound on top of these existing rates. China-made clothing faces the highest combined rates at 45%+, while clothing from other Asian countries faces 26-46% reciprocal tariffs.

Which brands are cheapest after tariffs?+

US-made brands avoid import tariffs entirely. Look for "Made in USA" labels from brands like American Giant, Todd Shelton, or some New Balance products. Second-hand clothing from ThredUp, Poshmark, or local thrift stores is also tariff-free since the original tariff was already paid.

Are luxury brands affected too?+

Yes, luxury brands face the same tariff rates based on country of manufacture. European luxury brands face 20% EU tariff, while brands manufacturing in China face 45%+. However, luxury brands have wider margins to absorb tariff costs.

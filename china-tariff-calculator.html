Understanding US Tariffs on Chinese Goods in

China faces the most complex and highest tariff rates of any US trading partner. The current tariff situation is the result of multiple overlapping trade actions that began during the first Trump administration trade war in 2018 and escalated dramatically in 2025.

How China Tariff Rates Are Calculated

The effective tariff rate on any Chinese product is a combination of several layers:

  • Pre-existing Section 301 Tariffs (2018-2019): These range from 7.5% to 25% depending on the product list (Lists 1-4A). Most consumer electronics, clothing, and toys were on List 4A at 7.5%.
  • IEEPA Tariffs (Feb-Mar 2025): An additional 20% tariff imposed under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act, citing fentanyl trafficking as a national emergency.
  • Reciprocal Tariffs (Apr 2025): An additional 34% "reciprocal" tariff announced on April 2, 2025, bringing the combined IEEPA + reciprocal total to 145% at peak.
  • Geneva Deal Reduction (May 2025): The US-China deal reduced the combined IEEPA + reciprocal portion to approximately 30%, while Section 301 duties remain on top.

The De Minimis Suspension: A Game-Changer

Perhaps the most impactful change for everyday consumers is the suspension of the de minimis exemption for Chinese shipments. Previously, packages worth under $800 could enter the US duty-free. Since May 2, 2025, this exemption no longer applies to goods shipped from China. This directly impacts millions of Americans who shop on platforms like Shein, Temu, and AliExpress. Even a $20 purchase from these platforms now faces full tariff rates or flat postal fees of $75-$150.

📋 Key Facts About China Tariffs

  • China is the largest source of US imports by value
  • Over $400 billion in annual Chinese imports are affected
  • De minimis ($800 duty-free) exemption is suspended for China
  • Section 301 duties apply ON TOP of the base 30% rate
  • Electronics, toys, and furniture from China are hardest hit
  • The average American household pays $800-$1,500 more due to China tariffs alone

Which Products Are Most Affected?

Electronics are the most impacted category, with effective rates of 54%+ on smartphones, laptops, and tablets from China. Toys and games face similar rates since over 80% of toys sold in the US are made in China. Clothing from China faces compounded tariffs of 45%+, though many brands have shifted production to Vietnam, Bangladesh, and India.

China Tariff FAQ

Common questions about tariffs on Chinese imports.

What is the current tariff rate on Chinese goods?+

The base tariff rate on Chinese goods is approximately 30% (post-May 2025 Geneva deal). However, Section 301 duties of 7.5-25% still apply on top for most product categories, bringing effective rates to 37.5-55%+ depending on the product. Some categories like EVs and semiconductors face even higher combined rates.

Are tariffs on China going to go down?+

The US-China tariff situation is fluid. The May 2025 Geneva deal reduced rates from 145% to ~30% base, showing that negotiations can produce results. However, the underlying trade tensions remain, and Section 301 tariffs have been in place since 2018. Future elections, trade negotiations, and geopolitical developments will all influence tariff levels.

Can I still order from Shein and Temu?+

Yes, you can still order from Chinese platforms like Shein and Temu, but you'll pay significantly more. With the de minimis exemption suspended, orders now face full tariff rates or flat postal duties of $75-$150 per package. Many shoppers find that small orders are no longer economical. See our Shein/Temu tariff calculator for specific estimates.

Do tariffs apply to products made in China but sold by US companies?+

Yes. Tariffs are applied based on country of manufacture, not the brand. An iPhone designed by Apple in California but assembled in China faces the full China tariff rate. This is why many companies are diversifying manufacturing to India, Vietnam, and other countries.

How do I check if my product is made in China?+

Check the product label or packaging for "Made in China" or "Made in PRC" markings. For online purchases, check the product listing details, or look at the customs declaration on the shipping label. Most electronics, toys, and fast fashion items are manufactured in China unless explicitly stated otherwise.

What's the difference between Section 301 and IEEPA tariffs?+

Section 301 tariffs were imposed in 2018-2019 specifically targeting unfair Chinese trade practices (IP theft, forced technology transfer). They apply to specific product lists at 7.5-25%. IEEPA tariffs were imposed in 2025 under emergency powers citing fentanyl trafficking. They apply broadly to ALL Chinese imports at a flat rate. Both apply simultaneously, which is why effective rates are so high.

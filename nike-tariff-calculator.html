Why Are Sneakers So Expensive Now?

The apparel and footwear industry faces some of the highest historical tariff rates, which were exacerbated by the 2025-2026 trade policy changes. Almost 0% of Nike footwear is manufactured in the United States, meaning nearly every pair of Nike shoes sold in America is subject to import tariffs.

📌 Where Nike Shoes Are Made (and Tariff Rates)

  • Vietnam (~50% of Nike shoes): Faces a 46% reciprocal tariff rate in 2026.
  • Indonesia (~25% of Nike shoes): Faces a 32% reciprocal tariff rate.
  • China (~20% of Nike shoes): Faces the highest combined tariff rate of 54%+.

How This Affects Retail Prices

If Nike imports a pair of Air Force 1s manufactured in Vietnam for a landed wholesale cost of $50, they must pay a 46% tariff ($23) to US Customs. This raises their wholesale cost to $73. To maintain standard retail margins, the final sticker price you see at the mall is raised significantly.

💡 How to Check Where Your Nikes Were Made

You can find the manufacturing country on the size tag inside the shoe (usually under the tongue or on the inner side). Look for "Made in Vietnam," "Made in Indonesia," or "Made in China."

Sneaker Tariff FAQ

Does Nike make any shoes in the USA?+

Currently, Nike does not manufacture any of its footwear in the United States. All Nike shoes are imported and subject to tariffs. They do manufacture some components (like the Air soles) in the US, but the final assembly happens overseas.

Why did Nike move production from China to Vietnam?+

Nike and other apparel brands began shifting production from China to Vietnam and Indonesia years ago to find lower labor costs and, more recently, to avoid the steep Section 301 tariffs applied to Chinese goods. However, the new reciprocal tariffs now impact Vietnam and Indonesia heavily as well.

Are Adidas and Puma affected by the same tariffs?+

Yes. Adidas, Puma, New Balance, and Under Armour rely on the same Southeast Asian supply chains as Nike. All imported footwear is subject to the same country-of-origin tariff rates.

US-Japan Tariffs Explained (2026)

Japan is one of the most significantly impacted US allies by the current tariff regime. As a close security partner, the imposition of tariffs has created diplomatic tension alongside economic costs for American consumers.

📌 Japan Tariff Key Facts

  • Reciprocal rate: 24% on most Japanese goods
  • Auto tariff: 25% on all vehicles (Toyota, Honda, Nissan, Subaru, Mazda)
  • Gaming: PlayStation 5 and Nintendo Switch face 24% tariff
  • Cameras: Canon, Nikon, Sony cameras face 24% duty
  • Japanese whisky and sake face the full 24% reciprocal tariff

Impact on Japanese Auto Brands

The 25% auto tariff is the single largest impact. A Toyota Camry assembled in Japan that cost $28,000 pre-tariff now costs approximately $5,600 more. Japanese automakers Toyota, Honda, Nissan, Subaru, and Mazda have all raised US prices. Some models assembled in US or Canadian plants may avoid the tariff.

Impact on Gaming & Electronics

Sony's PlayStation 5 Pro (assembled in Japan) faces a 24% tariff, adding ~$170 to its cost. Nintendo Switch 2 faces similar rates on Japan-assembled units. Canon and Nikon cameras, Sony audio equipment, and Panasonic electronics also face the full 24% reciprocal tariff.

Japan Tariff FAQ

How much is the tariff on Japanese cars?+

25% on all imported passenger vehicles. This applies to any Toyota, Honda, Nissan, Subaru, or Mazda assembled in Japan. Vehicles assembled in US plants (like some Toyota Camry and Honda Accord models) are not subject to this tariff.

Is the PlayStation 5 affected by tariffs?+

Yes. PlayStation 5 units assembled in Japan face a 24% reciprocal tariff. Sony has partially absorbed costs but retail prices have increased. Units assembled in China face even higher combined tariffs.

Are Japanese cars made in the US tariff-free?+

Yes, vehicles assembled in US plants are not subject to the 25% import tariff. Many Japanese automakers have US factories: Toyota (Kentucky, Indiana), Honda (Ohio, Alabama), Nissan (Tennessee), and Subaru (Indiana).

Will Japan negotiate a trade deal?+

Negotiations are ongoing. As a close security ally, Japan is considered a priority for a bilateral trade agreement. However, agricultural market access (rice, beef) remains a sticking point in negotiations.

What Is the De Minimis Rule?

The de minimis exemption (under 19 USC §1321) allowed packages valued under $800 to enter the US duty-free. This provision was originally designed to reduce customs processing costs for low-value shipments. However, it became the backbone of China-direct shopping platforms.

Timeline of Changes

  • Before May 2025: All packages under $800 entered duty-free regardless of origin
  • May 2, 2025: De minimis exemption suspended for Chinese shipments
  • Current: Chinese packages face full tariffs OR flat postal fees of $75-$150
  • Other countries: $800 de minimis threshold still applies for non-China shipments

Affected Platforms

  • Shein: Average order ~$50. Previously duty-free, now +$22-75+ in fees.
  • Temu: Average order ~$30. Previously duty-free, now +$13-75+ in fees.
  • AliExpress: Average order ~$25. Previously duty-free, now +$11-75+ in fees.
  • Wish: Average order ~$15. Previously duty-free, now +$6-75+ in fees.

De Minimis FAQ

Is the $800 duty-free rule still in effect?+

For most countries, yes — the $800 de minimis threshold still applies. However, for China, it has been suspended since May 2, 2025. All Chinese shipments, regardless of value, now face tariffs or flat postal fees.

Will the de minimis exemption come back?+

There is no announced date for reinstating the de minimis exemption for China. Given the ongoing trade tensions, it may remain suspended indefinitely. Some policymakers have proposed eliminating it for all countries.

What's the $75 postal fee?+

Instead of calculating ad valorem (percentage-based) tariffs on small packages, customs may apply a flat postal processing fee of $75 (via USPS) or $150 (via express carriers like FedEx/UPS). Whichever method results in a higher fee is typically applied.

Understanding US-Mexico Tariffs in 2026

Mexico is the largest US trading partner by total trade volume, with over $800 billion in goods crossing the border annually. The current 25% tariff was imposed under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA) citing fentanyl trafficking concerns.

📌 Mexico Tariff Key Facts

  • Current rate: 25% IEEPA tariff on most Mexican goods
  • USMCA exemption: Goods meeting USMCA (formerly NAFTA) rules of origin may qualify for 0% duty
  • Auto tariff: 25% on all imported vehicles regardless of USMCA status
  • Fresh produce: ~50% of US fresh fruits and vegetables come from Mexico
  • Beer impact: Corona, Modelo, Dos Equis, Pacifico all face the 25% tariff

Most Affected Mexican Imports

The categories most impacted by the US-Mexico tariff include:

  • Vehicles & Auto Parts: Mexico is home to major GM, Ford, Toyota, and VW assembly plants. The 25% auto tariff adds $7,000-$12,000 to imported vehicles.
  • Fresh Produce: Avocados, tomatoes, peppers, berries, and lettuce from Mexico face the 25% tariff, directly impacting grocery prices.
  • Beer & Spirits: Mexico is the largest beer exporter to the US. Corona, Modelo, and Dos Equis prices have risen 15-25%.
  • Tequila & Mezcal: Premium spirits from Mexico face the full 25% duty.
  • Electronics Assembly: Many electronics assembled in Mexican maquiladoras face tariffs on components.

🔄 USMCA Exemption

Products that meet USMCA (US-Mexico-Canada Agreement) rules of origin — meaning sufficient manufacturing or processing occurred in North America — may qualify for reduced or zero tariff rates. However, the IEEPA tariffs override USMCA benefits for many product categories. Check with a customs broker for specific product eligibility.

Mexico Tariff FAQ

Why are there tariffs on Mexican goods?+

The 25% tariff on Mexican imports was imposed under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA) in 2025, citing fentanyl trafficking concerns. It is not a reciprocal trade tariff but rather an emergency measure.

Does USMCA protect Mexican goods from tariffs?+

Partially. USMCA-compliant goods may qualify for reduced or zero duty rates, but the IEEPA tariffs override USMCA benefits for many product categories. The 25% auto tariff applies regardless of USMCA status.

How do Mexico tariffs affect grocery prices?+

Significantly. About 50% of US fresh fruit and vegetable imports come from Mexico. Avocados, tomatoes, peppers, berries, and lettuce have all seen price increases of 15-30% due to the 25% tariff.

Are Corona and Modelo beer prices going up?+

Yes. All Mexican beer exports face the 25% IEEPA tariff. Constellation Brands, which distributes Corona and Modelo in the US, has raised prices 15-25% to offset tariff costs.

Will Mexico tariffs be reduced?+

It depends on bilateral negotiations. The tariffs are tied to fentanyl enforcement cooperation. If Mexico demonstrates sufficient anti-trafficking measures, the administration has indicated willingness to reduce rates.

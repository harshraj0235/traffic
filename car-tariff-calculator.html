Understanding Auto Tariffs in

The automotive sector faces one of the most significant tariff impacts. A flat 25% tariff on all imported vehicles took effect April 3, 2025, followed by a 25% tariff on auto parts starting May 3, 2025.

Which Car Brands Are Affected?

  • Toyota, Honda, Nissan, Subaru, Mazda (Japan): Vehicles assembled in Japan face the full 25% tariff. However, many models are assembled in US plants (e.g., Toyota Camry in Kentucky, Honda Accord in Ohio).
  • BMW, Mercedes, Audi, VW, Porsche (EU): European-assembled luxury and mainstream vehicles face 25%. BMW's Spartanburg, SC plant produces some US models.
  • Hyundai, Kia (South Korea): Korean-assembled vehicles face 25%. Hyundai's Alabama plant produces some US models.
  • BYD, some Tesla (China): Chinese-assembled vehicles face 25% auto tariff plus additional China-specific tariffs, effectively pricing them out of the US market.

📋 Key Auto Tariff Facts

  • 25% tariff applies to ALL imported passenger vehicles and light trucks
  • 25% tariff also applies to auto parts (effective May 3, 2025)
  • US-assembled vehicles are NOT subject to the import tariff
  • Average new car price increase: $4,000-$12,000 due to tariffs
  • Used cars have also risen in price due to increased new car costs
  • Electric vehicles from China face the highest combined rates (50%+)

Car Tariff FAQ

Common questions about auto import tariffs.

How much is the tariff on imported cars?+

All imported passenger vehicles face a flat 25% tariff regardless of country of origin. This applies to vehicles assembled outside the US — even from allied nations like Japan, South Korea, and the EU.

Is my Toyota/Honda subject to the tariff?+

It depends on where it was assembled. Many Toyota and Honda models are assembled in US plants (Kentucky, Ohio, Indiana) and are NOT subject to the import tariff. Models assembled in Japan face the full 25% tariff. Check where your specific model is made.

Do auto parts also have tariffs?+

Yes. A 25% tariff on imported auto parts took effect May 3, 2025. This affects replacement parts, tires, and components, which increases repair and maintenance costs even for US-assembled vehicles that use imported parts.

Should I buy a used car instead?+

Used cars don't incur additional import tariffs (the tariff was paid on the original import). However, used car prices have risen due to increased demand from buyers avoiding tariff-inflated new car prices. Consider US-assembled new vehicles as an alternative.

Shein, Temu & AliExpress Tariffs Explained ()

If you shop on Shein, Temu, AliExpress, or Wish, the tariff landscape has changed dramatically. Here's everything you need to know about the new costs.

What Changed: The De Minimis Suspension

Before May 2, 2025, packages worth under $800 could enter the US duty-free under the "de minimis" exemption. This was the loophole that made ultra-cheap Chinese marketplace shopping possible. This exemption has been suspended for Chinese shipments.

Now, every package from China faces either:

  • Full tariff rates (45%+ for clothing, 54%+ for electronics)
  • OR flat postal fees of $75 (USPS) to $150 (express carriers) per package

Is It Still Worth Shopping on Shein/Temu?

For many shoppers, the math no longer works. A $30 Shein order that previously arrived duty-free might now face $13-$15 in tariffs (if processed via ad valorem rates) or a $75 flat postal fee — more than doubling the cost. Larger orders over $200+ may still offer some value since the tariff percentage stays the same regardless of order size.

📋 Key Facts for Shein/Temu Shoppers

  • $800 duty-free exemption SUSPENDED for Chinese shipments since May 2025
  • ALL orders now face full tariffs OR flat postal fees ($75-$150)
  • Clothing from China faces 45%+ effective tariff rate
  • Small orders are hit hardest — a $20 order may cost $95+ with flat fee
  • Larger orders are more economical (tariff is a percentage, not flat)
  • Some items may be shipped from US warehouses and avoid the worst fees
  • Shein and Temu are opening US/Mexico fulfillment centers to reduce impact

Alternatives to Consider

With tariffs making Chinese fast fashion less economical, consider domestic alternatives like Amazon Basics, Walmart's in-house brands, Target's Cat & Jack/A New Day lines, or secondhand platforms like ThredUp, Poshmark, and Depop. Check our tariff savings guide for more strategies.

Shein & Temu Tariff FAQ

Everything you need to know about tariffs on Chinese marketplace orders.

Can I still order from Shein in 2026?+

Yes, you can still order from Shein, but you'll pay significantly more. With the de minimis exemption suspended, all orders face full tariffs (45%+ for clothing) or flat postal fees of $75-$150 per package. Many shoppers find that small orders ($20-$50) are no longer economical.

How much will I pay in fees on a Temu order?+

It depends on the order size and products. Clothing orders face ~45% tariff rates (so a $50 order becomes ~$72.50). Alternatively, a flat postal fee of $75-$150 may apply per package. Temu is working to shift inventory to US warehouses to reduce these costs.

What happened to the $800 duty-free rule?+

The de minimis exemption (allowing packages under $800 to enter duty-free) was suspended for Chinese shipments on May 2, 2025. This was the mechanism that allowed Shein and Temu to sell ultra-cheap goods without duty charges. It remains suspended with no announced reinstatement date.

Do AliExpress orders also face tariffs?+

Yes. Any package shipped directly from China to the US faces the same tariff treatment regardless of the platform — Shein, Temu, AliExpress, Wish, DHgate, and Banggood are all equally affected by the de minimis suspension and China tariff rates.

Are there ways to avoid the tariffs?+

You cannot legally avoid tariffs on Chinese imports. However, some Shein and Temu items ship from US warehouses — these have already had tariffs paid and won't incur additional duties. Look for "Ships from US" labels. Alternatively, consider domestic shopping alternatives.
